What is Collaboratorium?

Collaboratorium
A community-powered creative laboratory.

This summer, artists, technologists, storytellers, designers, movers, facilitators, gamers, researchers, community builders, and curious humans come together around a shared challenge.

How might we create meaningful experiences in a world that feels increasingly fragmented especially at a distance?

Collaboratorium is a place to experiment with new forms of collaboration, contribute to a real-world project, build a cultural library of ritual interventions, and meet others who believe that art, technology, imagination, and community can shape a more connected future.

What does Collaboratorium mean?

Collaboratorium is an invented word that we use because none of the existing ones felt quite right.

It holds many ideas simultaneously — each one a small manifesto about how we want to work together.

What's next?

A step into the ancient future.

An interactive story for cross-cultural exchange.

A peace sanctuary at the foot of Mount Fuji.

October 2026
Prototype Gathering · Fuji Sanctuary, Japan
01

Fuji Sanctuary in Japan was created by Byakko Shinko Kai — a place where people from many nations, cultures, and faiths have gathered for nearly three decades in prayer for peace.

02

The heart of Fuji Sanctuary is a sacred field surrounded by the flags of the world, where people gather often to offer attention, intention, ritual and care as powerful, unseen forces in service to all beings on Mother Earth.

03

For the last 6 months, the Radiant IRIS collective and Byakko Shinko Kai have been wondering how to translate the essence of their Seven Station Ritual for World Peace into a form for the next generation and offer their wisdom cross-culturally.

04

The Collaboratorium will be another step toward the realization of this dream. We will offer the library of meaningful interactive rituals to Byakko Shinko Kai to see if any of the experiences can be in service for an October 2026 ceremony to repattern WWII energy.

05

One thousand and one moons ago, nations from around the world unleashed great destruction with the bombing of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. At this pivotal energetic juncture, we are again bringing cultures from around the world to unleash great creativity in service to revitalizing indigenous storytelling wisdom through modern technology.

06

The groups that create rituals selected for the October ceremony will be invited to participate in this offering if they wish. Radiant IRIS collective wishes this Collaboratorium to be the first of many that will eventually lead to in-person and online seasonal gatherings that will keep weaving cross-cultural communities together.

Questions & answers

Frequently
asked

  1. Complete the interest form.
  2. Receive an invitation to our Orientation gatherings.
  3. Meet the community, ask questions, and explore whether Collaboratorium feels right for you.
  4. After Orientation, decide whether you would like to participate in the ongoing gatherings.

Submitting the form does not commit you to participate. It simply helps us understand who is interested and how to design the experience together.

Collaboratorium is free to join. There is no cost to participate in the online laboratory.

Collaboratorium is a volunteer-based creative laboratory. There is no financial compensation. Participants gain access to a multidisciplinary creative community, collaborative projects, new relationships, and learning opportunities.

Participants are encouraged to attend the sessions and engage between as their schedule allows. There is flexibility in how deeply you choose to participate.

The sessions are designed to accommodate participants across Asia, Europe, and North America. Session times will be finalized after applications are reviewed.

Before the laboratory begins, all participants will receive clear information about how contributions, credit, and future use of materials will be handled. Our intention is a process that is transparent, fair, and aligned with the spirit of collaboration.

Every Collaboratorium seasonal cycle produces new ideas and prototypes, experience concepts, rituals, participation models, and creative partnerships. But perhaps more importantly: a deeper capacity to create together, relationships that continue beyond a single project, and a shared language among collaborators.
